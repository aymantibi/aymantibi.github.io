Work in Progress

  • Conditional Trade Policy for Peace? The Economic Effects of Qualifying Industrial Zones in the Middle East (Draft coming soon)
    Read Abstract

    How do conditional trade policies with input-sourcing requirements affect firm exports, production, and labor outcomes? To answer this question, I study Egypt’s Qualifying Industrial Zones (QIZ) program, which grants Egyptian firms duty-free access to the U.S. market conditional on sourcing inputs from Israel. I estimate event-study and triple-difference specifications using firm-level customs data, industrial census records, and labor force surveys. I find large and persistent export gains along both intensive and extensive margins alongside improvements in output, productivity, employment, and wages. The improvements are driven by U.S. market access rather than the quality of Israeli inputs. To map these firm-level responses into aggregate and welfare implications, I develop a quantitative heterogeneous-firm trade model with endogenous QIZ compliance, input requirement costs, and productivity upgrading. The framework is used to quantify the program’s economy-wide effects and conduct counterfactual analyses that vary the Israeli content requirement, highlighting the trade-off between lower tariffs and higher input costs.\end{abstract}
