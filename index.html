Work in Progress

  • Conditional Trade Policy for Peace? The Economic Effects of Qualifying Industrial Zones in the Middle East (Draft coming soon)
    Read Abstract

    This paper studies the economic effects of geographically targeted conditional trade policies through the case of the Qualifying Industrial Zone (QIZ) agreements in Egypt and Jordan. The agreement grants firms located within designated ares in Egypt and Jordan tariff-free access to the U.S market conditional on sourcing inputs from Israel. I find that treated firms increase their production, emploument, productivity, and exports. I quantify the general equilibruim effects of the QIZ policy using a heterogenous firm trade model, and then examine counterfactuals to shed light on the most effective design of such policies.
