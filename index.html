I am a PhD candidate in Economics at the University of Mannheim. My research focuses on international trade, development, industrial policy, public finance, and conflict.

I study policy-relevant economic questions with a regional focus on MENA, Africa, South Asia, and the EU. My work combines empirical analysis, large-scale data work, and quantitative general equilibrium models.

I collaborate with academic, policy, and private sector institutions to produce research with real-world impact.

Research

  • Conditional Trade Policy for Peace? The Economic Effects of Qualifying Industrial Zones in the Middle East
    Work in progress
    Abstract

    How do conditional trade policies with input-sourcing requirements affect firm exports, production, and labor outcomes? I study Egypt's Qualifying Industrial Zones program, which grants Egyptian firms duty-free access to the U.S. market conditional on sourcing inputs from Israel. Using firm-level customs data, industrial census records, and labor force surveys, I estimate the effects of the program on exports, output, productivity, employment, and wages. I also develop a quantitative heterogeneous-firm trade model with endogenous compliance and productivity upgrading to quantify aggregate effects and counterfactual policy scenarios.

  • The Political Economy of Newspaper Funding in India
    With Pooja Singh
    Abstract

    This project studies how incumbent governments in India allocate newspaper advertising and whether this spending is used strategically to influence electoral outcomes.
